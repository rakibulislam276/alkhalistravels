Best Family Holiday Destinations from Mumbai — With Prices & Packages

Planning a family holiday from Mumbai involves a different checklist than a couples’ getaway: flight times short enough for young children, hotels that don’t punish you for a family of four, food everyone will actually eat, and a budget that survives contact with reality. Having booked family trips for Mumbai households for two decades, here are the destinations that consistently deliver — with honest pricing.

1. Dubai, UAE — The All-Rounder

Flight time: 3h 30m direct  |  Family package: from ₹35,000 per person

Dubai remains the easiest international trip for Mumbai families: short flight, visa in 2–4 days, Indian food everywhere, and attractions across every age group — Aquaventure and the beaches for kids, malls and gold souks for parents, desert safari for everyone. Four to five days is the sweet spot. Travel October to March for outdoor-friendly weather.

2. Singapore — The Effortless One

Flight time: 5h 30m direct  |  Family package: from ₹45,000 per person

If you want a trip where nothing goes wrong, this is it. Universal Studios, Gardens by the Bay, the Night Safari and Sentosa fill four days easily; the city is spotless, safe for kids to roam, and Little India solves every food question. Singapore is pricier on the ground than its Southeast Asian neighbours — budget for attraction tickets in advance, they are the biggest expense.

3. Bali, Indonesia — Best Value for Longer Stays

Flight time: ~7h with one stop  |  Family package: from ₹48,000 per person

Visa-free entry for Indians and genuinely affordable villas make Bali the best-value week-long family trip. Base yourselves in Nusa Dua or Sanur for calm, child-friendly beaches (Kuta’s surf is for teenagers). Highlights for families: Waterbom water park, the Ubud monkey forest, and rice-terrace walks. Avoid the January–February monsoon.

4. Thailand: Phuket & Krabi — The Beach Classic

Flight time: 4h 30m direct to Phuket  |  Family package: from ₹40,000 per person

Direct flights, visa-free entry, and resorts that treat children as guests rather than nuisances. Island-hopping by longtail boat is the memory your kids will keep. November to April is the season; the sea is calmest for young swimmers then.

5. Sri Lanka — The Short-Haul Surprise

Flight time: 2h 30m direct  |  Family package: from ₹32,000 per person

Closer than many Indian domestic destinations, with beaches (Bentota), elephants (Pinnawala), and the train ride through tea country that children find as exciting as any theme park. Great value, easy visas, familiar food.

6. Europe: Paris & Switzerland — The Big One

Flight time: 9h+  |  Family package: from ₹65,000 per person (excluding Schengen visa)

The once-in-a-childhood trip. Disneyland Paris plus the Swiss Alps in 8–10 days is the classic itinerary. Plan 2–3 months ahead for the Schengen visa (every family member needs one, children included) and book summer travel by March for sane airfares.

Family booking tip: Always ask about child pricing — most international hotels let children under 12 stay free with existing bedding, and airlines price under-2s at roughly 10% of the adult fare. A well-structured family package prices each member correctly instead of multiplying the adult rate by four.

How to Choose by Age

  • Toddlers (under 5): Dubai or Sri Lanka — short flights matter more than anything
  • School-age kids: Singapore or Thailand — attractions and beaches in balance
  • Teenagers: Bali or Europe — enough independence and Instagram material
  • Multi-generation trips with grandparents: Dubai or Singapore — comfort, accessibility and food familiarity win

When to Book

For Diwali and summer school holidays — the two peaks for Mumbai families — book 8–10 weeks ahead; last-minute family bookings in peak season routinely cost 30–40% more across four tickets. October–November and February are the value windows, with good weather almost everywhere on this list.
