Schengen Visa from India: Requirements, Processing Times & Expert Tips

A Schengen visa opens 29 European countries with a single sticker — but it also has one of the most documentation-heavy applications Indian travellers face. Rules and border systems in Europe have been changing, and files that were fine a few years ago now get rejected on technicalities. Here’s the current picture and how to get approved the first time.

What’s Changed Recently

  • Digitalisation is rolling out: the EU is progressively moving Schengen applications online, though biometrics and VFS/consulate appointments remain part of the process for Indians for now
  • The Entry/Exit System (EES) has begun operating at EU borders, digitally registering non-EU travellers’ entries and exits — overstays are now automatically tracked, so respect your visa validity to the day
  • Cascade visas: Indian frequent travellers with a clean Schengen history can receive long-validity multi-entry visas (2-year, then 5-year) under the EU’s cascade regime — a strong reason to keep your travel record spotless
  • Appointment demand remains high: for summer travel, apply as early as possible — applications open up to 6 months before your trip

The Document Checklist

  1. Passport valid 3+ months beyond your return, issued within 10 years, with 2 blank pages
  2. Completed application form and 2 recent photos to Schengen spec (35×45mm, white background)
  3. Confirmed round-trip flight reservation and full hotel bookings covering every night
  4. Travel insurance with minimum €30,000 medical coverage valid across Schengen
  5. Last 3–6 months’ bank statements showing sufficient, stable funds
  6. Income tax returns (usually last 2–3 years)
  7. Employment proof: leave letter for employees; GST/company registration for business owners
  8. A cover letter explaining your itinerary, funding and ties to India
The rule of thumb consulates apply: they must be convinced you will return to India. Strong employment, family ties, property and a sensible, fully-funded itinerary answer that question before it’s asked.

Processing Times

The official standard is 15 calendar days, extendable to 45 in complex cases or peak season. Realistically, Indian applicants should budget 2–4 weeks after biometrics, plus the wait for an appointment slot itself. For a summer Europe trip, start your file 2–3 months out.

Top Rejection Reasons — and How to Avoid Them

  • Insufficient or inconsistent funds: large unexplained deposits right before applying look worse than a modest, steady balance
  • Itinerary gaps: every night must have accommodation proof; day-trip logic must match your bookings
  • Wrong consulate: apply to the country of your longest stay (or first entry if equal) — filing with the “easier” consulate is a classic rejection
  • Weak ties to India: unemployed applicants or those without clear return reasons need stronger supporting documents
  • Insurance shortfalls: policies below €30,000 or not covering all Schengen states are rejected on arrival at the counter

If You’re Refused

A refusal is not permanent. You may appeal to the refusing country within the stated window, or reapply with a corrected file — addressing the exact refusal ground cited in your letter. A professionally prepared reapplication succeeds far more often than an appeal.
