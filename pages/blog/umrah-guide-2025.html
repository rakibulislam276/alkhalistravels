Complete Umrah Guide: Everything You Need to Know Before Your Pilgrimage

Performing Umrah is one of the most spiritually significant journeys a Muslim can undertake. For first-time pilgrims travelling from India, good preparation makes the difference between a stressful trip and a serene, focused pilgrimage. This guide walks you through every stage — from paperwork in Mumbai to the rituals in Makkah.

1. Umrah Visa Requirements for Indian Citizens

Indian passport holders need a valid Umrah visa issued through a licensed operator. You will need:

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months from your travel date, with at least two blank pages
  • Passport-size photographs with a white background
  • Confirmed return ticket and hotel bookings in Makkah/Madinah
  • Vaccination records as per current Saudi health requirements (meningitis ACYW135 is standard; check the latest advisory before travel)

When you book through a registered operator, the visa, tickets, hotels and ground transport are arranged together — which is also what Saudi regulations expect for pilgrim travel.

2. Best Time to Perform Umrah

Umrah can be performed year-round outside the Hajj season. For pilgrims from Mumbai:

  • November to February offers the most comfortable weather (20–30°C) but higher crowds and prices
  • Ramadan carries immense spiritual reward — the Prophet ﷺ said Umrah in Ramadan equals Hajj in reward — but expect peak crowds and book 3–4 months ahead
  • May to August is hottest (45°C+) but quietest and most economical

3. Packing Checklist

Essentials

  • Ihram (2 sets for men), comfortable abayas/modest clothing for women
  • Unscented soap, toothpaste and moisturiser (scented products are prohibited in the state of Ihram)
  • Comfortable, well-broken-in sandals and a drawstring shoe bag for the Haram
  • Universal power adapter, power bank, and a small day bag
  • Medicines with prescriptions, ORS sachets, and basic first aid
  • Photocopies of passport and visa kept separately from originals

4. The Rituals of Umrah, Step by Step

  1. Ihram & Niyyah: Enter the state of Ihram at the Miqat (pilgrims flying into Jeddah typically assume Ihram before boarding or at the airport) and make the intention for Umrah, reciting the Talbiyah.
  2. Tawaf: Circle the Holy Kaaba seven times anti-clockwise, beginning at the Black Stone corner.
  3. Two rakats at Maqam Ibrahim followed by drinking Zamzam water.
  4. Sa’i: Walk seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah.
  5. Halq or Taqsir: Men shave or trim their hair; women trim a fingertip’s length. This completes the Umrah and releases you from Ihram.
Tip: Perform Tawaf on the upper floors during peak hours if you are elderly or travelling with family — it takes longer but is far less crowded and safer.

5. Choosing Hotels Near the Haram

Hotel distance is the single biggest factor in your daily comfort. Within 500m of the Haram you can return to your room between prayers; beyond 1km you will rely on shuttle buses, which get very busy at prayer times. Economy packages typically use hotels 700m–1.5km away with shuttle service; premium packages place you within walking distance. Decide based on the mobility of the eldest member of your group.

6. Practical Tips for First-Timers

  • Take a local SIM or eSIM at Jeddah airport; data is cheap and essential for staying connected with your group
  • Keep your operator’s Saudi contact number saved offline
  • Hydrate constantly — carry a refillable bottle for Zamzam
  • Learn the duas beforehand rather than reading from a phone during Tawaf
  • Budget for Saudi Riyals in cash for small purchases; cards are widely accepted in malls and hotels

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days do I need for Umrah?

Most packages from Mumbai run 10–15 days, split between Makkah and Madinah. The Umrah rituals themselves take only a few hours — the remaining days are for prayers in the two Holy Mosques and ziyarat.

Can women travel for Umrah without a mahram?

Saudi Arabia now permits women to perform Umrah without a mahram, subject to current regulations. Group travel with a registered operator is strongly recommended.

What does an Umrah package from Mumbai cost?

Economy packages start around ₹72,000 per person including direct flights, visa, 3-star hotel and transport, rising with hotel proximity to the Haram and travel dates.
